1. Valley of the Kings in Luxor

The Valley of the Kings, located on the west bank of the Nile near Luxor, is a burial site for pharaohs and nobles from Egypt's New Kingdom (18th-20th Dynasties). It contains 65 tombs, including famous ones like that of Tutankhamun, and features elaborate decorations that offer insights into Egyptian mythology and afterlife beliefs. The valley includes the East Valley, where most royal tombs are, and the West Valley, also known as the Valley of the Monkeys. Despite most tombs being looted in antiquity, the site remains a significant archaeological and historical location. Designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1979, it continues to be an area of active exploration and research.

Address: Valley of the Kings, West Bank, Luxor Governorate, Egypt
