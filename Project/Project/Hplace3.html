3. Hurghada Museum

The Hurghada Museum is the first antiquities museum in the Red Sea Governorate and a groundbreaking achievement in Egypt's cultural landscape. It's a product of collaboration between the Ministry of Tourism and Antiquities, the Red Sea Governorate, and the private sector, with the museum's construction funded by private investors. Inside, you'll find over 2,000 artifacts that span Egypt's rich history, from the Ancient Egyptian, Greco-Roman, Jewish, and Christian periods to the Islamic and Modern eras. The museum's theme focuses on showcasing the concept of beauty throughout Egypt's diverse history, making it a must-see for history and culture enthusiasts.
