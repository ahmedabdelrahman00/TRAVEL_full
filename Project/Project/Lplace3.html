3. The Karnak Temple

The Karnak Temple Complex, located near Luxor, Egypt, is a vast site made up of temples, pylons, chapels, and other structures. Construction began during the reign of Senusret I in the Middle Kingdom (c. 2000-1700 BC) and continued into the Ptolemaic Kingdom (305-30 BC), although most of the buildings today date from the New Kingdom. Karnak was the principal religious center of the ancient Egyptian Theban Triad, with the god Amun at its head. The complex is part of the ancient city of Thebes and was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1979. It gets its name from the nearby village of El-Karnak, located 2.5 kilometers (1.6 miles) north of Luxor.
